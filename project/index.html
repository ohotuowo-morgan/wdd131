Understanding Web2 vs Web3

The internet has evolved from centralized platforms (Web2) to a decentralized future (Web3).
Here's a quick look at what makes them different.

Web2: The Centralized Web

In Web2, data and content are controlled by major companies like Google, Meta, and Amazon. Users rely on centralized servers and have little control over their personal data.

Web3: The Decentralized Web

Web3 is built on blockchain technology. It allows users to own their data, trade digital assets directly, and interact through decentralized applications (dApps).

Key Differences

  • Ownership: Web2 = Companies own data, Web3 = Users own data
  • Structure: Web2 = Centralized, Web3 = Decentralized
  • Currency: Web2 = Traditional money, Web3 = Crypto and tokens
